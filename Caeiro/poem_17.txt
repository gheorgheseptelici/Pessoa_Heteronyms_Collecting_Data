XVII Salad What a medley of Nature ﬁlls my plate! My sisters the plants, The companions of springs, the saints No one prays to... And they’re cut and brought to our table, And in the hotels the noisy guests Arrive with their strapped-up blankets And casually order “Salad,” Without thinking that they’re requiring Mother Earth To give her freshness and her ﬁrst-born children, Her very ﬁrst green words, The ﬁrst living and gleaming things That Noah saw When the waters subsided and the hilltops emerged All drenched and green, And in the sky where the dove appeared The rainbow started to fade...
