 The leaf won’t return to the branch it left Nor form a new leaf with the same stem. The moment, which ends as this one begins, Has died forever. The vain and uncertain future promises No more than this repeated experience Of the mortal lot and the lost condition Of things and of myself. And so, in this universal river Where I’m not a wave, but waves, I languidly ﬂow, with no requests And no gods to hear them.
