from part one / coat of arms COAT OF ARMS: THE CASTLES Europe, stretched out from East to West And propped on her elbows, stares From beneath her romantic hair With Greek eyes, remembering. Her left elbow is pulled back; Her right forms an angle. The ﬁrst, which lies ﬂat, says Italy; The second says England and extends The hand that holds up her face. She stares with a fatal, sphinxian gaze At the West, the future of the past. The staring face is Portugal.
